ORGANIZATIONAL RULES

OF EDUCATIONAL AND HEALTHCARE ENTITY: MODERN SCHOOL OF PSYCHOTHERAPY SP. Z O.O. - The owner of brand i e- counselling clinic: - MENTAL RECOVERY

§ 1. General provisions

  1. The organizational regulations were established pursuant to Art. 24 in connection with Art. 23 of Act from 15th of April, 2011 on medical activities (consolidated text: Journal of Laws of 2023, item 991, as amended) in Poland as it is location of entity.

  2. The Regulations specify the method and conditions of providing health and educational services by the medical and educational entity operating under the name Modern School of Psychotherapy spółka z ograniczoną odpowiedzialnością with its registered office in Kraków, address: ul. Karłowicza 23/6, 30-347 Kraków, KRS: 0001104856, NIP: 6772510371, hereinafter referred to as the "Entity" or "Service Provider") in Poland as it is location of entity.

  3. The rules apply to all employees of a given entity, as well as staff providing medical services to it - under civil law contracts, patients, students, visitors and other entities staying on the premises of the medical facility or entity in connection with the process of providing health and educational services.

§ 2. Goals and tasks of the entity

  1. The purpose of the Entity's activities is to provide patients with outpatient and/or health services by taking actions aimed at maintaining, saving, restoring or improving health, as well as conducting other medical activities resulting from the treatment process or separate regulations regulating the principles of their implementation. Additionally, the Entity is involved in promoting health and providing health education in the field of psychotherapy and psychological support.

  2. The tasks of the Entity are:

  3. Providing education in the field of psychotherapy and various forms of psychological support.

  4. Providing basic health services in the field of psychiatry.

  5. Providing psychotherapeutic services and psychological consultations, with particular emphasis on:

    • Providing health services in accordance with the latest medical knowledge, respecting patient rights, and in accordance with ethical principles and applicable law;

    • Promoting a healthy lifestyle and conducting health education activities for patients;

    • Participation in the education process of people preparing to work in medical professions and people already working in the industry by organizing practical internships and improvement courses;

    • Carrying out other tasks resulting from applicable local legal provisions and related to the activities of the Entity

§ 3. Type and scope of provided educational and health services

  1. The entity conducts medical activities, providing health services on an outpatient basis.

  2. The entity specializes in providing outpatient health services in the field of psychiatry, psychological counseling and psychotherapy, including in particular:

  3. Conducting medical consultations;

  4. Providing psychological counseling and psychoeducational therapy;

  5. Issuing decisions and certificates regarding patients' health status;

  6. Issuing prescriptions for necessary medications;

  7. Activities promoting health and health education of patients;

  8. Organizing psychoeducational classes;

  9. Conducting training and courses in the field of medicine consistent with the Entity's business profile;

  10. Improving skills and education in the field of psychotherapy, psychology, art therapy and similar, as well as improving professional qualifications in this field

  11. The entity conducts educational activities by providing educational services in the field of psychotherapy and psychological support via online and stationary meetings

§ 4. Organizational structure

  1. The entity carries out medical and educational activities through the operation of a medical facility and an educational unit under the name Modern School of Psychotherapy Sp. Z. o. o., which is the owner of the Mental Recovery brand.

  2. The structure of the medical facility and the educational unit referred to above includes:

  3. An organizational branch called the Mental Health Clinic - Mental Recovery brand, which operates: Specialized medical unit - Mental Health Clinic;

  4. An organizational branch called the School of Psychotherapy in the integrative trend Modern School of Psychotherapy, which operates: Specialized educational unit - School of Psychotherapy in the integrative trend

  5. Within the organizational structure of the medical unit - Mental Health Clinic and the structure of the educational unit - School of Integrative Psychotherapy - the following areas of activity of the Entity are separated:

  6. Medical practice in the field of psychiatry (especially: psychiatric consultations for adults, children and adolescents);

  7. Psychological and psychotherapeutic services (especially: psychological consultations, individual therapy for adults, children and adolescents, group therapy, psychological training and courses, and others);

  8. Administrative and financial services (in particular: accounting, management, organizational matters, all things related to staff and payroll, secretarial management);

  9. Educational services in the field of psychotherapy, psychology and similar;

  10. Other independent workstations.

  11. Management of the Entity's activities, including the above-mentioned The entity and its subordinate organizational units are managed by the Entity's management board as the Entity's manager (hereinafter referred to as the "Manager").

  12. The Manager is responsible for managing the Entity's affairs, representing it externally and performing other duties arising from applicable law.

  13. The manager has the right to appoint deputies to manage individual units or organizational units and to conduct specific types of matters. For this purpose, it may grant its deputies the necessary powers of attorney or authorizations.

  14. If further organizational units and/or organizational units are established, they will be formally defined by updating the Regulations and will cooperate to ensure the efficient functioning of the Entity, as well as the availability, comprehensiveness, continuity and appropriate quality of the health and educational services provided.

§ 5. Place and course of providing health and educational services

  1. The registered office of the Entity is Kraków

  2. The places where health and educational services are provided are offices run by our employees, places chosen by our employees, and those specified on the Internet in the company's media.

  3. Health and educational services are provided at hours agreed with employees, as well as their patients and students. Patient admissions take place on dates agreed with the patient or his legal representative in the manner specified in this paragraph.

  4. Health services may be provided in the form of personal stationary visits or remote consultations (e-visits).

  5. Patients can register in person, by phone or online using various platforms or applications.

  6. The registration process, payment and appeal procedures or changes to the online visit via various websites or applications, as well as the rules for remote consultations (e-visits) are set out in the regulations for the provision of services electronically or orally or by SMS.

  7. During stationary or telephone registration, online, the patient must pay the full fee for the visit in accordance with the price list of health and educational services within the time agreed with a given employee.

  8. During stationary or telephone registration, the patient or his legal guardian must provide his/her personal data, including name and surname and/or PESEL number and/or e-mail address and/or other identity document, telephone number and accept the terms and conditions of the regulations.

  9. If additional fees arise during the provision of services, the patient will be informed and payment for these services must be made no later than on the day of the visit.

  10. In the case of telephone registration, the Service Provider sends a link to make a payment to the e-mail address or telephone number provided by the patient. Payment may be made via PayU, PayPal, Blik, bank transfer or using available means of payment to the Entity's account.

  11. The Entity accepts the following forms of payment during stationary registration or for additional services referred to in section 10:

  12. Cash;

  13. Bank transfer;

  14. Credit/debit card;

  15. BLIK, PayU, PayPal payments, etc.

  16. Registration in any form indicated in section 5 above is tantamount to the Patient's consent to the provision of health services and acceptance of these Regulations.

  17. Before starting the provision of health services, the Patient is obliged to provide the staff with all necessary information and explanations that are necessary for the proper performance of the services, in particular to provide the required data for the medical documentation and to present the medical documentation and test results.

  18. Before the first visit, the Patient will receive an information clause regarding the processing of his personal data.

  19. The conditions for the provision of health services include: a. registration of the visit in accordance with the provisions of the Regulations, b. payment for services before the visit, c. provision by the Patient of the information referred to in section 14, d. positive qualification of the Patient, which takes place after meeting the above conditions, subject to the provisions of § 7 section 3 of the Regulations.

§ 6. Cancellation or change of visit date

  1. Free cancellation or change of appointment date is possible no later than 24 hours before the scheduled visit.

  2. To cancel or change the date of your visit, please send an e-mail to the address provided in the Regulations or cancel in the calendar. Other methods of canceling or changing your appointment are not accepted.

  3. Failure to show up for an scheduled appointment or failure to cancel on the scheduled date (section 1) will result in the loss of the entire prepayment, regardless of the reason.

  4. Changing the appointment date later than 24 hours before the scheduled visit will result in loss of 50% of the prepayment, regardless of the reason. The 50% prepayment will be refunded under the conditions specified in section 7.

  5. If the Patient is late for an appointment, the visit will be shortened by the time of delay, without changing the amount of the fee. If the delay exceeds half of the scheduled visit time, the Service Provider has the right to cancel the reservation, which results in the loss of the prepayment, which is non-refundable, regardless of the reason for the delay.

  6. The service provider has the right to cancel the booked appointment date no later than one hour before its start. If the visit is canceled, the Service Provider will offer the Patient a new date. If the Patient does not accept the new date or it is not proposed, the Service Provider will refund the entire prepayment to the Patient.

  7. The prepayment will be refunded by the Service Provider within 7 days from: a. the date of cancellation or change of the booked appointment date in accordance with section 1 (100% refund of the prepayment), b. the day of changing the appointment date by the Patient without observing the conditions specified in section 1, by canceling the previous date and registering for a new date (50% refund of the prepayment), c. the day of cancellation of the booked visit date by the Service Provider in accordance with section 6 (100% refund of the prepayment), to the bank account number from which the payment was made, or in the case of cash or payment card payment in the office, to the bank account number provided by the Patient. The patient may be charged with the costs of commissions and fees incurred by the Service Provider in connection with payment for the visit via PayU or a bank in the case of a foreign transfer.

  8. With the consent of both Parties, the amount of prepayment due for refund referred to in section 7 lit. b), can be counted towards the next visit. Then, upon registration, the patient is obliged to pay a fee of 50% of the visit price.

§ 7. Availability of health services

  1. The entity organizes health services in a way that ensures the best possible availability and the most convenient form of using the services for patients.

  2. Health services are provided to patients against payment, according to the price list applicable on the day the service is provided, established by the Manager:

  3. Psychiatric consultation: PLN 200-800 (UP TO EURO 199)

  4. Psychological-intervention-mentoring consultation: PLN 150 - 800 (UP TO EURO 199)

  5. Psychotherapy consultation/session: PLN 179-800 (UP TO EURO 199)

  6. Group session: PLN 99-PLN 500 (UP TO EURO 99)

  7. The entity reserves the right to refuse to admit the Patient or to stop providing health services in the following cases: a. when the Patient's health condition makes it impossible to provide health services, b. in the event of failure to meet the conditions specified in § 5 section 16, c. in the event of an epidemic threat or for reasons of health safety of patients; d. situations when the patient, his legal representative or persons accompanying the patient violate the obligations and prohibitions specified in § 10 of the Regulations. The decision on this matter is made by the Manager, his deputy or another person authorized by the Manager; e. in other cases when the provision of health services is impossible for reasons beyond the control of the Entity.

§ 8. Conclusion of contract of an e-visit/ electronic visit

  1. The contract for the provision of an e-visit or stationary visit is concluded after the Patient has successfully booked the date.

  2. Reservation of an appointment is made by completing the registration form and selecting the appropriate date in the schedule available on the website in the "Make an appointment" tab or on the website by selecting a specialist marked as a Forma Poradnia specialist.

  3. When booking a visit, the patient must: a. choose a specialist, date and type of visit (stationary or remote); b. complete the registration form, providing the required data: name, surname, PESEL number (in the absence of PESEL, type and number of the identity document), residential address, telephone number and e-mail address; c. accept these Regulations; d. make payment for the visit in accordance with the price given when selecting the date of the visit, in accordance with the applicable price list.

  4. Booking a visit or e-visit via the website does not require creating a patient account.

  5. Providing data by the Patient is tantamount to a declaration that all information provided is true and that the Patient has full legal capacity or, if it is limited, has the consent of the legal guardian.

  6. After completing the registration form, a link to the website enabling prepayment will be sent to the e-mail address provided by the Patient.

  7. The condition for booking an appointment is to make a prepayment. The prepayment is deemed to have been made after it has been credited to the Service Provider's bank account or after the payment has been confirmed by the PayU website.

  8. Failure to pay the prepayment up to 48 hours before the selected visit date, or immediately in the case of booking a date less than 48 hours before the visit, will result in the cancellation of the reservation, and the date becomes available to other patients.

  9. Prepayment can be made using available payment channels on the website in the "Make an appointment" tab or on the website.

  10. Information about the prepayment and confirmation of the reservation or its cancellation due to lack of prepayment will be immediately sent to the e-mail address provided in the registration form.

  11. Receiving confirmation of the reservation means that the Patient has successfully signed up for a visit or e-visit. Upon receipt of confirmation, the contract between the Service Provider and the Patient for a visit or e-visit is concluded.

§ 9. Quality of health services

  1. The Entity's staff provides health services with appropriate qualifications and authorizations in accordance with separate regulations.

  2. Health services are provided to patients with the utmost care, in accordance with current medical knowledge, respect for patient rights and in conditions that meet applicable professional and sanitary requirements.

  3. Medical staff are guided by the principles of professional ethics defined by the relevant professional self-governments.

§ 10. Medical documentation

  1. The entity maintains, stores and makes available medical records in accordance with the Act of November 6, 2008 on patient rights and the Patient Ombudsman and implementing regulations.

  2. The entity ensures the protection of personal data contained in medical documentation, which is stored for the period specified in applicable law.

  3. The Entity charges fees for providing medical records in accordance with the price list set out in paragraph 7. 2.

§ 11. Patient's rights and obligations

  1. Health services are provided with respect for patients' rights specified in the Act of November 6, 2008 on patients' rights and the Patient Ombudsman and in separate regulations.

  2. A patient who believes that his rights have been violated while using health services has the right to submit an oral or written complaint to the Manager or his/her representative.

  3. The patient has the right: a. to receive reliable and conscientious diagnostic, treatment and rehabilitation services from persons with appropriate professional qualifications, in accordance with the principles of professional ethics; b. obtain medical care in conditions that respect his personal dignity, privacy, religious beliefs and moral principles; c. be informed about your health condition in an understandable way, and in the event of an unfavorable prognosis - the information should be provided tactfully and carefully. At the patient's request, the doctor may not disclose complete information. Informing other people about the patient's health condition requires his consent, unless the law provides otherwise or obtaining consent is not possible; d. expect health care workers to maintain professional secrecy; e. participate in the selection of the attending physician; f. participate in making informed therapeutic decisions regarding his health by obtaining full, clear and understandable information about potential risks and benefits, the possibility of using alternative treatment and the consequences of refusing the proposed medical procedure.

  4. The patient is obliged to: a. comply with the provisions of the Regulations; b. take care of personal hygiene; c. respect the rights of other patients; d. take care of the Entity's property.

  5. The patient is obliged to respect the equipment and current property of the Company. If damage is caused by his fault, the patient must cover the cost.

  6. The entity is not responsible for clothing, valuable items or money left unattended by the patient on the premises of the Center.

§ 12. Rules of cooperation with other medical entities

  1. In order to ensure the correct and uninterrupted provision of health services, the Entity cooperates with other entities providing medical services that offer medical services to patients, as well as with professionals from various fields of medicine.

  2. This cooperation is carried out in accordance with applicable law and with respect for patient rights. Detailed terms of cooperation may be specified in separate agreements.

§ 13. Final provisions

  1. The Regulations are effective from the date of registration of the Entity in the National Court Register, i.e. from May 13, 2024.

  2. In matters not regulated in these Regulations, the provisions of applicable law shall apply, including in particular the Act of April 15, 2011 on medical activities and the Act of November 6, 2008 on patient rights and the Patient Ombudsman, along with their acts. executives.
